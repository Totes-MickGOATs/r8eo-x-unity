# RC Tire Tread Patterns

Reference for R8EO-X tire selection system. Part of the [RC Tire Guide](./rc-tire-guide.md).

---

## Pin Tires (Mini Pins / Micro Pins)

Small cylindrical rubber pins (2-5mm tall) arranged across the tire surface.

- **Surfaces:** Hard-packed clay, dusty tracks, blue groove, indoor clay
- **How they grip:** Each pin deforms independently. Round pins provide omnidirectional grip.
- **Pin height matters:**
  - Short pins (2-3mm): Hard-packed, minimal loose dirt. More precise steering.
  - Medium pins (4-5mm): General-purpose, good balance.
  - Tall pins (6mm+): Loose or loamy conditions.
- **Key principle:** "The lower the pin, the more precise the steering; the larger the pin, the more grip." (Adam Drake)
- **Examples:** Pro-Line Hole Shot, JConcepts Double Dees, AKA Pin Stripe

## Bar Tires (Ribs / Bar Treads)

Horizontal connected bars running across the tread, sometimes at angles.

- **Surfaces:** High-grip indoor clay, blue groove, carpet, treated/prepped surfaces
- **Why bars vs pins:** Maximum contact patch; connected tread = more consistent traction; bars resist side-bite better; longer-lasting.
- **Trade-off:** Bars pack with mud/dirt on loose surfaces; pins self-clean better
- **Examples:** Pro-Line Electron, JConcepts Ellipse, AKA PinStripe (bar/pin hybrid)

## Step Pin Tires

Staggered/tiered pin designs with two heights.

- **Surfaces:** Loose dirt, loamy, transitional (wet-to-dry)
- **Advantage:** Dual-layer construction: tall pins dig into loose material while short pins provide secondary contact. Better structural support for tall pins. Better dirt evacuation.
- **Examples:** JConcepts Step Spike, JConcepts Drop Step, JConcepts Pin Swag

## Knobby / Lug Tires

Large, widely-spaced aggressive tread blocks.

- **Surfaces:** Very loose dirt, mud, sand, grass, wet, heavily rutted tracks
- **How they work:** Deep treads physically dig into soft material. Maximum mechanical grip.
- **Characteristics:** High rolling resistance; self-cleaning; poor on hard-pack (lugs fold over)
- **Examples:** Pro-Line Trencher, JConcepts Goose Bumps

## Slick Tires

Smooth, treadless rubber surface.

- **Surfaces ONLY:** Carpet, astroturf, blue groove, tarmac/concrete, treated indoor clay
- **How they work:** Maximum rubber-to-surface contact. Grip comes entirely from adhesion.
- **Notable trend:** Multiple manufacturers moved toward slick/near-slick designs for indoor racing.

## Hole Shot Tires

Pro-Line's iconic multi-pin design (3rd generation, Hole Shot 3.0).

- **What they are:** Dense, small-pin tire optimized for hard-pack versatility.
- **Why popular:** Excellent blend of forward AND side bite; wide condition range; consistent as track evolves.
- **Best for:** Hard dirt, dusty hard-pack, hard-pack with loam on top
- **Not ideal for:** Grass, heavy mud, very loose conditions

## Other Notable Patterns

| Pattern | Characteristic | Best Conditions |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Dirtwebs (JConcepts) | Web-like connected tread | Medium-grip outdoor tracks |
| Chainlinks | Connected chains of pins | Loose-to-medium conditions |
| Sprinter (JConcepts) | Directional side-pin | Outdoor dirt oval racing |
| Rehab (JConcepts) | Dense pin, bar-like behavior | High-grip outdoor with dust tolerance |
| Switchblade (Pro-Line) | Directional, different inside/outside | Tunable via mounting direction |
| Positron (Pro-Line) | Bar/pin hybrid, directional | Indoor clay, maximum forward bite |

## Front vs. Rear Tire Differences

**2WD Buggy:**
- Front: Narrow (50mm), ribbed/directional — unpowered, designed for cornering
- Rear: Wide (65mm), full tread pattern for power delivery

**4WD Buggy:**
- Front: Medium (58mm), same tread as rear — powered fronts need matching traction pattern
- Rear: Wide (65mm), full tread

**Wheel spec:** All 1/10 buggies use 2.2" rim diameter with 12mm hex mounting.
