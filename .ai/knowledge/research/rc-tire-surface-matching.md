# RC Tire Surface Matching

Surface-to-tire selection guide for R8EO-X. Part of the [RC Tire Guide](./rc-tire-guide.md).

---

## Surface Selection Guide

### Packed Clay (Outdoor, Groomed)

Most common competitive RC surface.

- **Tread:** Small-to-medium pins (Hole Shot 3.0, JConcepts Sprinter)
- **Compound:** Medium or Soft depending on moisture (M3/Blue when watered, M2/Gold when dry)
- **Why:** Hard surface needs smaller pins for precision. Medium compound balances grip and wear.

### Loose Dirt (Dusty, Ungroomed)

Low-traction conditions requiring maximum mechanical grip.

- **Tread:** Tall pins or step pins (JConcepts Double Dees V2, JConcepts Goose Bumps)
- **Compound:** Super Soft / Soft (M4/Green cold; M3/Blue warm)
- **Front tires:** Ribbed fronts (2WD) help cut through dust to find grip

### Indoor Clay

Typically higher grip than outdoor due to controlled moisture and temperature.

- **Tread:** Bar tires or small pins (Pro-Line Electron/Positron, JConcepts Ellipse)
- **Compound:** Gold (clay-specific), Aqua, or MC
- **Key difference from outdoor:** Indoor tracks often treated/prepped. Use bar/connected tread — not "edgy" in high traction.

### Carpet / Astroturf

Indoor surfaces with consistent, high-grip characteristics.

- **Tread:** Spike tires (Schumacher Minispike for astroturf); Slicks or near-slicks for short-pile carpet
- **Compound:** Medium carpet-specific compounds (Schumacher Yellow dry, Silver wet)
- **Pile height matters:** Tall pile → longer spikes; short pile / astroturf → shorter spikes/mini-pins; flat carpet → slick

### High-Grip "Blue Groove" Clay

Racing line becomes polished with deposited rubber — extreme grip.

- **Tread:** Very small pins or "taper pins" / "fuzzy" tires
- **Compound:** Soft to Medium (slightly harder than regular clay — too-soft compounds become "edgy" on deposited rubber)
- **Principle:** "The closer and finer the tread pattern, the more it should be used for blue groove."

### Wet / Damp Clay

Moisture dramatically changes grip characteristics.

- **Tread:** Larger pins or step pins (to cut through water film)
- **Compound:** One grade softer than dry conditions. Pink clay compound (AKA) for damp sticky clay.
- **Key changes:** More aggressive tread; some patterns "pack up" with wet clay; vent holes important for drainage.

### Hard-Packed with Rocks/Debris

Abrasive surfaces that destroy soft compounds quickly.

- **Tread:** Medium pins with durability (Pro-Line Hole Shot in S-series compound)
- **Compound:** Long-wear variants (S3/S5, AKA Long Wear)
- **Why:** Abrasive surfaces provide mechanical grip. Long-wear compounds trade peak grip for 2-3x longer life.

### Sand

Very loose, deep, non-compacting surface.

- **Tread:** Paddle tires or very aggressive knobby/lug patterns
- **Compound:** Hard — tire scoops material, not adhesion
- **Note:** Sand is fundamentally different — grip comes from displacing material, not friction. Very high rolling resistance.

### Tarmac / Asphalt

Hard, smooth, high-grip surface.

- **Tread:** Slicks or very low-profile pins
- **Compound:** Soft to Medium depending on temperature and roughness
- **Note:** Similar to blue groove but without rubber deposit.

### Grass

Soft, variable surface with root structure.

- **Tread:** Aggressive knobby (Pro-Line Trencher specifically designed for grass)
- **Compound:** Medium — needs structure to dig through grass blades to reach soil
- **Key:** "In grass, the Holeshots are not optimal; for those conditions the Trenchers will be your weapon of choice."
