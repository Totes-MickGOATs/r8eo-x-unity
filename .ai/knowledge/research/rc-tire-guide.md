## Quick Reference: Surface → Tire + Compound

| Surface | Tread | Compound |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Packed clay (dry) | Small pin / Hole Shot | M2 or Gold |
| Packed clay (watered) | Small pin / Hole Shot | M3 or Blue |
| Loose dirt | Tall pin or step pin | M4/Green (cold), M3/Blue (warm) |
| Indoor clay | Bar tires or small pins | Gold, Aqua, or MC |
| Carpet / astroturf | Slick or spike | Medium carpet-specific |
| Blue groove | Very small / fuzzy pin | Soft to Medium |
| Wet clay | Larger / step pins | One grade softer than dry |
| Sand | Paddle or knobby | Hard |
| Tarmac | Slick | Soft to Medium |
| Grass | Aggressive knobby (Trencher) | Medium |

## Key Principle

**"The harder the surface, the softer the tire; the softer the surface, the harder the tire."**

Grip on hard surfaces comes from rubber adhesion — soft compound maximizes adhesion.
Grip on soft surfaces comes from mechanical tread digging in — soft compound would fold.
